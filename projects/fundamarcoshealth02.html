FUNDAMARCOS Health: Dental Hygiene

In Guatemala the introduction of soda pop, cheap candy, and little plastic bags of frozen colored sugar water has been terrible for children's teeth. In the remote parts of Baja Verapaz where FUNDAMARCOS is working, these sugary snacks were not being brought in until 10 to 15 years ago. The unavailability of dental care, plus little or no tooth brushing compound the situation. Now children have more cavities than the adults.

It is easy to skip brushing children's teeth when a tooth brush costs one-forth of a man's daily wage, and no one has ever emphasized the importance of brushing. For a week in November 2010, a dental hygienist from Colorado taught 612 enthusiastic people, 2/3 of whom were children, to brush their teeth correctly. Because these families were all participants in FUNDAMARCOS's ongoing agricultural and nutrition programs, attendance and participation were great. Everyone was given a tooth brush and taught to make their own tooth paste. Mary McKay and local volunteers complimented the classes with a "No Junk Foods" drama.

Manuela Ramos, assisted by Juan Ceballos, are providing dental hygiene follow-up in 2011. They and the whole staff are also continuing to emphasize cutting out soda pop and sugary junk foods, and instead eating the vegetables and fruits the families are now producing. FUNDAMARCOS has also arranged with a very cooperative local pharmacist to sell quality tooth brushes at an affordable price.
