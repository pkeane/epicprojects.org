Agricultural Training Center
La Semilla del Progreso
Honduras

Youth Groups:

La Semilla del Progreso is presently providing agricultural vocational education for groups of youth in four villages. Through a 2-year structured program of instruction, La Semilla is giving teens from farming backgrounds new hope and the necessary skills to someday support their own families in rural Honduras. These youth of 14 to 22 years old are responding with enthusiasm, embracing the idea of developing their own agricultural projects. Having their own projects, they are putting in many hours of labor and are beginning to obtain the kind of harvests that will encourage them to continue and to take on new projects. The agricultural training and inspiration provided by La Semilla has been able to restore the term "farmer" as a viable vocational option for these teenagers. Success is also due to the fact that parents, grandparents, and often siblings are actively involved in the program. For example, it is the parents, or sometimes grandparents, who provide these young people with parcels of land to put into practice their newly learned farming knowledge.

TRAINING SCHEDULE

During the first year of training, the youth receive two 4-day residential courses at the La Semilla Training Center, with follow-up classes in the local community and individual visits to the project of each student by Laureano Jacobo or other staff of La Semilla. Each youth group is also guided by a part-time junior leader. This is a former student who has demonstrated enthusiasm and has had excellent agricultural projects. In the second year, the staff of La Semilla conducts all the classes and group meetings in the village. They also regularly visit all of the students individual projects to give encouragement and instruction.

Since the program with youth begun in 2001, La Semilla del Progreso has mentored approximately 4 groups a year, working with each group 2 to 3 years. When one group graduates, La Semilla begins a new group in a new area. In addition to the students participating in each year's program, La Semilla keeps in contact with many past students and occasionally provides them special addition courses or learning opportunities.
